Machine Learning — the tiny tour

Concept

Patterns, not programs

ML discovers rules from examples. Instead of hand-coding every rule, we fit models that generalize from data.

Practice

Train → Validate → Test

Split data, tune on validation, hold out a final test. Reliable evaluation beats lucky results.

Fun fact

Bias–Variance vibes

Too simple → misses patterns; too complex → memorizes noise. Sweet spot = best real-world performance.

Bioinformatics — data into discovery

From raw sequences to interpretable signals: QC, alignment, counting, and normalization turn biology into tidy tables that ML can learn from.

  • Features: genes, transcripts, variants, pathways
  • Normalizing: TPM, VST, TMM — scale data for fair comparisons
  • Reproducibility: workflows make results reliable & shareable

Friendly glossary

  • Feature matrix: rows = samples, columns = features.
  • Label: what we want to predict (e.g., tumor type).
  • Cross-validation: multiple train/test splits to estimate performance.

Cancer classification — signals of origin

Idea

Tissue “accents”

Gene expression carries a tissue accent — useful for telling cancer types apart when models listen carefully.

Approach

From signatures to scores

Turn thousands of genes into compact signatures, train a classifier, and score how confidently it recognizes each class.

Trust

Robustness matters

Evaluate across splits and cohorts. Calibrate probabilities so high scores actually mean higher odds.
